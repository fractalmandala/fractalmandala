The first cakravartin may well have been a simple head of villages, his leadership bringing nothing more than the standardisation of farming practices. The point of extracting history from literature is not to find stories of individuals, but to reveal the broad strokes of ancient Indian history. How did the early tribes and dynasties arise? Was early civilisation self-emergent, or led by the actions of a few leaders? In what ways did technological advancement in trade, transport and metallurgy change society? What was the involvement of dharma in all of this, and where do the Vedas figure in the chronology? Who composed them, who compiled them, and under what conditions? These are the kinds of questions we want answers to, and we use the cakravartins as milestones in our narrative.
